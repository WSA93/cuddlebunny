Before You Stay

Important Boarding Info

We ensure a meticulous, luxurious, and safe environment for every bunny in our care. Here is everything you need to know about our boarding routines and accommodations.

Accommodations & Comfort

Single boarded bunnies will be in a carpeted 2’ x 6’ exercise pen and bonded pairs (upon request) will be in a 4’ x 6’ carpeted exercise pen. We will provide a litter pan, ceramic water & food bowls, greens (organic romaine, cilantro, flat parsley, spring mix, and baby bok choy), and often a cardboard hidey box. Let us know if you do not want cardboard provided.

Pet parents are asked to bring items of their choice that have their bunny’s scent (toys, bowls, blankets, small tunnels) to help them adjust. Please be sure these items are not so large that they take up the entire ex-pen!

Diet & Hydration

We stock Oxbow adult & young bunny pellets that are provided at no extra charge. Because a sudden switch in pellets can cause tummy upset, we ask pet parents to bring in a supply of pellets to last the length of their bunny’s stay if they eat any other brand.

The bunnies are given room temperature Brita filtered water. If your bunny requires distilled water for medical reasons, please let us know and we are happy to provide that as well.

Monitoring & Updates

Each enclosure has a care sheet clipped to the side to help us track scheduled feedings, cleanings, requested nail trims, and medications. Food & water intake as well as fecal output is closely monitored.

While we cannot promise daily updates, we will do our best to send several throughout your bunny’s stay (typically the day after arrival and every couple of days thereafter). If you are an anxious pet parent, let us know! We're happy to update you more frequently for your peace of mind.

Safety & Routines

Boarded bunnies do NOT interact with our bunnies nor with other boarded bunnies, and are never brought upstairs. Music is played throughout the day to keep them entertained while staff members spend time interacting with them.

Each day before we depart, every bunny gets a small treat (blueberries, apple, dried cranberry, or a grape). We can easily substitute a digestive, urinary support, or papaya tablet if you prefer. As we turn off the lights, we routinely say, "Goodnight sweet bunnies!" Bunnies thrive on routines, and we want them to thrive.

Schedule & Drop-offs

Our day begins at 9am and ends around 9pm. Drop-off and pick-up is preferred between 10am-8pm, with the exception of Wednesdays when we are closed. Special arrangements need to be made outside of these standard times and on Wednesdays.

*NOTE: We are closed on Thanksgiving, Christmas, and New Year's Days. Arrangements for pick-up need to be made either the day before or the day after these holidays.
